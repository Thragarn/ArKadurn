Once the masters of the powerful Kingdom of Haradbar, dwarves resemble humans that grew in width rather than height. This race is composed of masterful craftsmen and resilient warriors, guided by unshakable honor and a strong loyalty towards clan and family.
The fall of Haradbar following the war with the Murd'Asyl coalition left them deeply bitter, particularly towards elves and Ogroids, although moon elves and gremlins (who largely migrated along with them) maintain the lesser and softer part of this centuries-old grudge.

- [b]Stone Dwarves[/b] compose the majority of this race, their ancestors having chosen exile to the Hirimgaard and Drascor over occupation. They now mostly live on the surface and have become the soul of the taverns of the North, drinking heavily and sharing stories of their adventures using the most coarse language any race could use.

- [b]Grey Dwarves[/b] chose to remain in their ancestral homes, refusing to abandon their stone halls to the occupation of the rising Drow empire. Although they eventually became full citizens in their new nation through the integration and unification efforts of its emperors, Grey Dwarves live in constant, simmering resentment. They are noticeably less chatty and grimly aggressive, their bodies and minds having been hardened by two centuries lived in the volcanic depths of Eretyrn.

Female dwarves having beards is, of course, a gremlin myth that offends some and amuses most. But if we are to mention their capillarity, female dwarves have the most luscious and thick manes among hominids, rivalled only by werewolves, maybe (or weresheep).
