Similar to humans, elves are tall, elegant, and pointy-eared humanoids. Their taste for arts and all things refined, high attunement to magic, combined with the general beardlessness of their male population gave them a false reputation of physical weakness, though this is far from being the case.
They are as diverse as humans, and can be divided into three subraces:

- [b]High Elves[/b] are the most common variant, descending from the noble families of the old civilisation. They are the most widespread of their kind, found primarily in Silmaran, but also in great numbers in Drascor, Feijen, Ashelha and Hirimgaard.

- Recognizable by their silver hair, [b]Moon Elves[/b] are rare and widely viewed as a peaceful,  nature-loving people with a great penchant to healing professions. They are the least adventurous subrace, sticking to their ancestral home forests for the most part.

- Said to be descendants of the first dopplers, [b]Drow[/b] have violet skin that is said to help them sneak in the shadows, white or ink black hair, and eyes that shimmer in dim lighting. They suffer from a long-standing reputation of being perfidious, though much of this prejudice is due to ancient history and their failed coup in Silmaran. Following their expulsion from the fae kingdom two centuries ago, they moved north-eastwards to lay foundation to Murd'Akhar. Today, they are mainly found in northern Caeros: Hirimgaard, Drascor, and Murd'Akhar, where their greatest numbers reside.
