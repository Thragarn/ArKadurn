Tall, slender, and naturally grumpy, Goblins are the reclusive survivalists of the ogroid family. They possess a vast array of physical traits, with skin shifting from green to yellow or red, and head structures ranging from flat to elongated. However, that doesn't mean some aren't attractive by human standards. These Ogroids are notoriously isolationist and hard to befriend, and their keen senses make the crowded stone walls and artificial noise of cities extremely overwhelming, leading to urban disorientation. 

Having formed a profound bond with the natural world around them, goblins can speak with and understand any animal, and possess a high natural resistance to lethal toxins. Consequently, they dwell in "Living Cities" made of bone and vines within the canopies of the Murd'Akhar jungles. While they begrudgingly joined the empire for safety, they tend to dislike the Drow’s manipulative methods and the Orcs’ extreme violence, and serve as the empire’s best outriders and scouts. Goblins that never left their jungles remain suspicious of most allies and typically only listen to the Gremlins, whom they consider their closest kin.
