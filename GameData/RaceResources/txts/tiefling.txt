As the Urudshahag  civil war unfolded, not everyone rose against their king; some loyalist factions had interests in his rule, or truly believed in his ways.
According to ancient scriptures, the Spirit of Flesh took anger and broke his neutrality, painting their skin red like the blood they shed and growing them horns to symbolise their guilt. The Fire Spirit took pity and made them the gift of his own element to restore equilibrium and salvage their dignity.

Following their sovereign's defeat, the first tieflings fled to the Hirimgäard, a harsh region of frozen volcanic lands north of Eretyrn. There, their bodies adapted to gain more strength, size, and resilience, with their males reaching orkish sizes.
This environment shaped them into a fierce and adventurous people, who value great hunts and duelling feats, serving as the core population of the northern nation.
The current Flesh Spirit no longer holds that ancient grudge and this tale has been, for better or worse, mostly forgotten.
