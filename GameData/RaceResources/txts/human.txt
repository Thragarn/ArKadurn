The most polyvalent, adaptable and polyvalent of all the races of the continent, humans claim to be the closest descendants of the ancient Hominids.
History remembers them as a paradox, as the greatest heroes who saved our world along with the most cruel villains who sought to destroy it were mostly humans.
Humans are diverse in appearance, but they truly differ little. Nonetheless, they can be categorized into three major cultures:

- The [b]Caeorans[/b], or [b]Caeorn[/b], are the most numerous humans. Native to the supercontinent Caeros, their history is a chronicle of endless wars, shaping cultures that value chivalry, discipline, and ambition.

- The [b]Yugin[/b] dwell in the southern lands of Caeros called the Feijen lands, far beyond the steppes of the Kahans. Living in secular, independant communities that only unite in the face of great, existencial threats, their cultures value freedom, the wisdom of the journeyman, and hard work, shaping them into master craftsmen and peerless merchants.

- The [b]Nagani[/b], known to traders as [b]the people of Nag[/b], designate themselves as the [b]Aatun[/b] meaning "those who arrive". They are the descendants of the hominids that followed the prophet Hashuram on his exodus to the the new continent of Nagaradh. Nagani cultures are scholarly and deeply spiritual, flourishing in a relative peace far from the never-ending conflicts of Caeorn.
