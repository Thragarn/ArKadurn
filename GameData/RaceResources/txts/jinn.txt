Initially thought by scholars to have lived in complete isolation atop the highest spires of the Vhaatur mountains, the Jinn's true history is written on their own skin and in the stone walls of their temples. Their ancestors were among the travelers who followed Hashuram, but while most reached the continent of Nagaradh, the Jinn’s vessel drifted into a rift to the plane of Nercana. As they spent centuries cast from one plane to another, their bodies and culture were reshaped: Nercana turned their skin blue and their hair white and floaty, while the grueling trials of each subsequent taught them to harness the powers of raw mana.

Once they finally returned to the original plane on the peaks of Vhaatur, they did not realize the trial was over. Treating the freezing summits as yet another hostile realm, they built temples and villages with a grim resolve, only to discover through their first contact with the populations of Feijen that their odyssey was finally over. The Jinn then set out to repopulate the lands they had lost, mostly inhabiting Feijen but also journeying out to Ashelha to finally reach the land their ancestors had sought.
The Nagani humans recognize the Jinn as their true brothers, perhaps even more so than other humans.

Though Jinn are calm and versatile by nature, their bodies are living maps. They channel their mana through intricate tattoos that are unique to each member, marked by the revered Inkers, keepers of the ancient art.
