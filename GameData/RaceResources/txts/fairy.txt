Tiny, whimsical and perpetually youthful, Fairies are the remnants of the ancient Hominid nobility and scholars called the Fae. While most call the marble cities and sunny forests of Silmaran home, their restless nature draws them to Drascor, Feijen, Ashelha, and the exotic islands of Lycoria. Wherever they live, they often act as messengers or masters of precision crafts such as intricate clockwork or high cuisine.

Hyperactive by nature, Fairies rarely touch the ground, flying around with translucent bee-like wings that hum with magic rather than flapping. This magical potency serves as their greatest asset, as fairies compensate for their physical fragility with unmatched mana output to the point that even Ogres and Giants avoid provoking one.
Fairy temperaments are famously binary, being either uncontainable balls of sunshine or cold, resentful enemies with hearts of ice.
History bears witness to the Fairies' power, as the Drow coup against the crown of Silmaran would have succeeded had the Fairies not intervened. In the oral traditions of the Dark Elves, these tiny beings are remembered as merciless tricksters and terrifyingly ruthless mages who turned a rightful revolt into a carnage.

Fairy society is widely seen as matriarchy due to the rarity of males, called Sylphs. Accounting for only one in six births, Sylphs are seen as signs of good fortune and are married into multiple mother-centered households upon reaching adulthood at thirty.
Fairies age at a glacial pace, only appearing middle-aged as they approach their third century.
